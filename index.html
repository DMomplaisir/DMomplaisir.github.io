about me

Being born and raised in Queens, New York, I've spent my life listening to people's stories, whether it be a woman on the train busking, or my parents telling their stories from Haiti. I have a love for authentic narratives, and find myself in all of my projects trying to provide platforms for people to tell their stories, and develop new connections. Whether it be through designing applications for civil activism, or developing a photography project based on developing a less warped "image world" - I find myself trying to provide a space for people to tell their tale.

I’m Dylan Momplaisir, a front-end web developer, user experience design enthusiast, and photographer, currently attending Amherst College. I’ve won awards for advertising campaigns for teaching, startup funding for a teen tech incubator, and for a small documetary on Kurdistan.

If you interested in working on projects where your product needs to scream "authenticity”, contact me below.
